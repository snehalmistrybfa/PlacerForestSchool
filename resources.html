Nature Activities for Home

Simple ways to bring forest school principles into your daily life

Backyard Nature Exploration

Create opportunities for discovery in your own outdoor space, no matter how small

  • Set up a 'sit spot' for quiet observation
  • Create nature art with found materials
  • Start a nature journal or photo collection
  • Build with sticks, stones, and natural materials

Seasonal Nature Activities

Connect with the changing seasons through outdoor exploration

  • Collect and press autumn leaves
  • Build snow sculptures and ice decorations
  • Plant seeds and watch them grow in spring
  • Create summer nature mandalas

Urban Nature Connections

Find nature opportunities in city environments

  • Visit local parks and green spaces
  • Observe urban wildlife and birds
  • Explore community gardens
  • Take nature walks in your neighborhood

Getting Started with Nature-Based Learning

Simple steps to begin incorporating outdoor learning into your family life

1

Start Small

Begin with short outdoor explorations in your backyard or local park

2

Follow Your Child's Lead

Let your child's interests guide the exploration and discovery

3

Embrace Mess and Discovery

Allow for dirt, water play, and hands-on investigation

4

Ask Open-Ended Questions

Wonder together: 'What do you notice?' 'What happens if...?'

5

Be Patient

Allow time for deep observation and unhurried exploration
